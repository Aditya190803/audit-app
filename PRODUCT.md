# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Employees of Shah Kapadia & Associates (theska.in), a CA firm. They use the app at their desks during audit engagements to analyze bank statement PDFs against client lists. Context: high-stakes compliance work, long review sessions, need for precision and trust. Primary task on any screen: review transactions, verify tags, and export findings.

## Product Purpose

A desktop tool that ingests bank statement PDFs and client CSVs, automatically tags transactions via fuzzy matching and rule engines, and enables efficient manual review with full audit trail and flexible export. Success means faster audit turnaround with fewer missed anomalies.

## Brand Personality

Trusted. Precise. Uncluttered. The brand of a 15-year-old CA firm that calls itself "Your Partner in Growth" — expert without arrogance, warm without being casual.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS dashboard clichés (big-number hero metrics, gradient text, identical card grids).
- Bloated enterprise software with dense chrome and excessive borders.
- Dark-mode-everything or neon-on-black crypto aesthetic.
- AI-generated slop UI (glassmorphism defaults, side-stripe borders, modal-first flows).
- Overly playful or cartoonish fintech looks.

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity under pressure**: every element must reduce cognitive load during long review sessions.
2. **Authority through restraint**: generous whitespace, muted palette, confident typography; no decorative noise.
3. **Progressive disclosure**: surface the most important signal first, reveal detail on demand.
4. **Trust the process**: the UI should feel as reliable as the firm behind it — no surprises, no ambiguity.
5. **Respect the document**: bank statements are the center of attention; the interface should frame them, not compete.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG 2.1 AA. Reduced motion respected. Color is never the sole signal for tags (shape + text always present). High-contrast mode friendly.
